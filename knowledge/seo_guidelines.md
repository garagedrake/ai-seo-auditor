# Official SEO Guidelines (2026)
**Last updated:** 2026-03-22

This knowledge base contains the latest guidelines for search engine optimization, developed with a special focus on AI summaries, user intent, and technical performance, validated through official Google criteria and industry-leading sources.

## 1. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) & AI Overviews
The dominant strategy for 2026 is to optimize for LLMs (Large Language Models) and AI Overviews (SGE).
* **Atomic Answers:** Pages must provide direct, clear "TL;DR" answers to the user's intention very early in the content to be extracted.
* **Information Gain:** Google now prioritizes "information gain" extremely highly. Content must add original research, unique data points, or genuine personal experience. Simple summaries of existing facts are ranked down or ignored by the AI summaries.
* **LLM Accessibility:** The site should offer structures intended for AI crawlers (e.g., `llms.txt`) and adapted Schema markup to facilitate parsing. Only 38% of AI citations now come from the regular Top 10 list, which means Google looks for hidden experts ("hidden gems") that are properly structured.

## 2. Technical Performance & Core Web Vitals (CWV)
Core Web Vitals have been integrated even deeper and adapted for AI.
* **Instant Page Experience for AI:** Speed is now also judged based on how quickly the page can be rendered and read in "AI Mode" for real-time synthesis.
* **Rendering:** Clean and parsable DOM structure without bloated Javascript is crucial for AI crawlers to quickly extract relevant quotes.

## 3. Multi-Platform E-E-A-T & User Intent
* **Conversation Continuity:** A page's value is judged not only by the keyword, but how well it can meet the user's entire "journey" in a conversation. Anticipating follow-up questions on the page leads to higher visibility.
* **Earned Media:** "Authority" is now strongly measured through third-party commentary. Brand signaling, social proof, and consistency across the web are crucial.
